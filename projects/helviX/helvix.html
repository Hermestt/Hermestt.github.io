From a single and complex file to a kanban like structure

By creating a kanban-like structure using Figma Teams, I started by helping helviX move away from a single design file to an easier and lightweight way to look and work with design mockups.

Read more about it in this blog post

The results were good, the separation of topics made it easier to navigate and find what we were looking for. Loading files and navigating was free of lag and overload. Also the files with the handoff designs were free of clutter.

Quotes & Dynamic quotes

One of the main features of helviX platform is the Quote feature where the steel dealer makes an offer to the buyers request. An addition to this feature is to allow the buyer to accept only specific articles from the quote.

Additional Costs

As in every industry where logistics exists, we have additional costs besides the the price of the products we’re buying. After some iterations we found that keeping it simple and invoice-like was the best way to display these costs to the users in a clear manner.

Blanket Orders

The steel industry has these annual contracts that the buyers and sellers set up. We’ve revamped the interface and experience where these contracts visible to enable the users to look for articles inside those contracts easier and effortlessly, just as they do for regular shop articles.

Previously we had only a visualization of the contracts with the materials inside, but after listening to our users we’ve learned that having a shop view of these materials would help the users to find materials that they had already negotiated with the seller to purchase on a fixed rate.
